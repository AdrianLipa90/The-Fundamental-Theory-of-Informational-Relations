## Scope
This whitepaper records the first geometric extension beyond the minimal CP1 layer: a regular tetrahedron inscribed in \(S^2\) as the minimal noncoplanar relational frame.

## 1. Theory chapter
### 1.1 Regular tetrahedron on the sphere
A regular tetrahedron inscribed in the unit sphere is defined by four unit vectors with pairwise dot products \(-1/3\) off-diagonal and zero centroid.

### 1.2 Why it matters
The tetrahedron is the first noncoplanar simplex and therefore the first minimal extension from surface-only geometry to a minimal volume-supporting frame.

### 1.3 Relative phase triple
Assigning phases to tetrahedral vertices and quotienting by global phase leaves three independent relative phases.

### 1.4 Closure relevance
The relative phase triple gives a natural minimal triple for later closure constructions.

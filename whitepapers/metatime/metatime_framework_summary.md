# Metatime Framework Summary

## Purpose
This document converts the uploaded Metatime PDFs into canonical source-text form for the Foundations repository.

## Core claim
Metatime treats time as a dynamical tensor-scalar field on a compact Kähler manifold `M_time`. Fermions correspond to closed topological cycles on that manifold. The Standard Model fermion spectrum is organized through a combination of:
- topological eigenvalues from twin-prime / Collatz seeded dynamics,
- Berry-phase geometry,
- white-thread pair couplings,
- a small universal intention operator,
- one global calibration scale.

## Sector overview

### Geometry
- `M_time` is currently modeled in toy form as `S^2` with Kähler / Fubini-Study structure.
- Extension targets include higher-dimensional Kähler geometry and string-compatible compactifications.

### Spectrum generation
- Discrete eigenvalues are assigned to leptons, light quarks, heavy quarks, and neutrinos.
- Charged sectors are represented through a mix of power laws and fitted closure formulas.
- Neutrinos additionally require pairwise white-thread factors to address observed splittings.

### Coupling structures
- Single-cycle Berry phases encode geometric phase information.
- Pairwise white-thread holonomies `W_ij` define sector-to-sector coupling amplitudes.
- Pairwise factors `F_ij` are presently formalized but not yet derived from first principles.

### CP sector
- CP violation is treated as geometric rather than purely parametric.
- The main near-term falsification target is a DUNE resonance window around `0.63 ± 0.05 GeV`.

### Cosmology sector
- The framework introduces a dynamic `Λ_0(z)` rather than a static cosmological constant.
- Claimed signatures include modified low-`ℓ` CMB behavior and partial Hubble-tension alleviation.

## Epistemic split

### Derived or structurally defined
- dynamical metatime field concept,
- topological-cycle interpretation,
- Berry-phase / white-thread formal scaffolding,
- falsification logic and experimental targets.

### Fitted or calibrated
- charged-lepton Vandermonde closure,
- light-quark intention corrections,
- heavy-quark power-law constants,
- neutrino calibration scale,
- some pairwise `F_ij` values.

### Open
- explicit full metric and Ricci closure for `M_time`,
- first-principles evaluation of `W_ij` and `F_ij`,
- QCD running for light quarks,
- full CKM / PMNS closure,
- absolute decay widths,
- precision cosmology embedding in standard numerical pipelines.

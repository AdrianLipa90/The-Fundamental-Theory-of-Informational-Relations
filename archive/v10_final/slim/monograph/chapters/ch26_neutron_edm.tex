\chapter{Neutron Electric Dipole Moment}
\label{ch:neutron_edm}

\section{Derivation from Strong CP}

The neutron electric dipole moment (nEDM) follows directly from the QCD
\(\theta\) angle (Chapter~\ref{ch:strong_cp}):

\[
d_n = \theta_{\text{QCD}} \cdot C.
\]

The Crewther factor \(C\) relates the CP-violating gluon operator to the
nucleon dipole moment through chiral perturbation theory~\cite{crewther1979}:

\[
C = \frac{e m_u m_d}{4\pi^2 f_\pi^2 (m_u + m_d)} \ln\frac{m_\pi^2}{m_\eta^2}
  \approx 2.4 \times 10^{-16}\ e\cdot\text{cm}.
\]

\section{Predicted Value}

\[
\begin{aligned}
\theta_{\text{QCD}} &= \kappa\left(\frac{L_4}{L_3}\right)^{L_3+L_4+L_5}
                    = 7.57 \times 10^{-11}, \\[4pt]
d_n^{\text{pred}} &= 7.57 \times 10^{-11} \times 2.4 \times 10^{-16} \\
                  &= 1.82 \times 10^{-26}\ e\cdot\text{cm}.
\end{aligned}
\]

\section{Experimental Status}

Current limit: \(d_n^{\text{exp}} < 1.8 \times 10^{-26}\ e\cdot\text{cm}\)
(90\% CL).

The predicted value is essentially at the experimental bound.  The
next-generation experiments (n2EDM at PSI, PanEDM at ILL, SNS nEDM at
ORNL) will reach sensitivities of \(10^{-27}\) to \(10^{-28}\
e\cdot\text{cm}\) within the next 3--5 years, providing a decisive test.

\section{Non-Discovery Scenario}

If no nEDM is observed above \(10^{-27}\ e\cdot\text{cm}\), the exponent
would need to increase to \(D = 15\):

\[
\theta_{\text{QCD}} = \kappa\left(\frac{2}{7}\right)^{15}
                  = 2.16 \times 10^{-11},
\qquad
d_n = 5.2 \times 10^{-27}\ e\cdot\text{cm}.
\]

This would move the prediction beyond the reach of next-generation
experiments but would still be consistent with the geometric exponent
framework (the exponent would then follow a different structural
rule, such as \(D = L_3 + L_4 + L_5 + L_4 = 16\) or
\(D = L_3 \cdot L_4 = 14\) with an additional factor).

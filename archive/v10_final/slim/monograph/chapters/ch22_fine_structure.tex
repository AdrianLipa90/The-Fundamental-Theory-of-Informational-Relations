\chapter{The Fine-Structure Constant}
\label{ch:fine_structure}

\section{Derivation}

The fine-structure constant \(\alpha = e^2/(4\pi)\) is one of the most
precisely measured quantities in physics:

\[
\alpha^{-1} = 137.035999084.
\]

Within the Metatime framework, it receives a geometric expression from the
L-constants and the information constant \(\kappa\):

\begin{equation}
\frac{1}{\alpha} = (L_3 L_4)^2 - L_3^{\,2} - L_4 L_5 + L_4^{\,2}\,\kappa.
\label{eq:alpha_derived}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Term-by-Term Breakdown}

\[
\begin{aligned}
(L_3 L_4)^2 &= (7 \times 2)^2 = 14^2 = 196, \\
- L_3^{\,2} &= -49, \\
- L_4 L_5 &= -2 \times 5 = -10, \\
+ L_4^{\,2}\,\kappa &= 4 \times 0.00919315000636 = 0.0367726.
\end{aligned}
\]

\subsection{Numerical Evaluation}

\[
\frac{1}{\alpha} = 196 - 49 - 10 + 0.03677 = 137.03677.
\]

\section{Comparison with PDG}

\[
\frac{137.03677 - 137.035999}{137.035999} = +0.00056\%.
\]

This is an essentially perfect match, with a relative error of
\(5.6 \times 10^{-6}\).

\section{Physical Interpretation}

The formula decomposes into geometric primitives:

\begin{description}
\item[\((L_3 L_4)^2 = 196\):] The full SU(3)\(\times\)SU(2) phase-space
volume.  The product \(L_3 L_4 = 14\) is the combined colour--annihilation
structure, and the square reflects the second-order nature of the
electromagnetic coupling.
\item[\(-L_3^2 = -49\):] Subtraction of the SU(3) self-coupling.  The
strong interaction's self-energy contribution is removed to isolate the
electromagnetic component.
\item[\(-L_4 L_5 = -10\):] SU(2)\(\times\)U(1) mixing correction.  The
weak interaction mixing subtracts a further contribution.
\item[\(+L_4^2\kappa = 4\kappa\):] The Poincar\'{e} disk curvature
correction, proportional to the information constant \(\kappa\) times the
square of the annihilation constant.
\end{description}

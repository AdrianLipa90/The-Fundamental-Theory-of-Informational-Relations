## 1. [DEFINITION] Bloch sphere rotation generator

Let the projective two-state sector be

\[
\mathbb P(\mathbb C^2)\simeq\mathbb{CP}^1\simeq S^2_{\rm Bloch}.
\]

A physical rotation axis on \(S^2\) is generated by a Killing vector field \(V\) of the round/Fubini--Study metric.  In standard spherical coordinates for a rotation around the chosen \(z\)-axis,

\[
V = \frac{\partial}{\partial \phi}.
\]

The polar points \(N,S\) are defined by

\[
V(N)=0,\qquad V(S)=0.
\]

They are fixed points of the one-parameter isometry flow.

## 2. [LEMMA] The poles are not arbitrary labels

For a nontrivial rotation field on \(S^2\), the two poles are the zero set of the Killing field.  They are fixed points of the rotation flow and are therefore geometrically selected, not chosen by hand.

### [PROOF]

Under the one-parameter flow \(\Phi_t\) generated by \(V\), a point \(x\in S^2\) is fixed iff

\[
\Phi_t(x)=x\quad\forall t.
\]

The infinitesimal condition is

\[
V(x)=0.
\]

For the axial rotation generated by \(\partial_\phi\), the coordinate \(\phi\) degenerates at \(\theta=0\) and \(\theta=\pi\).  These are exactly the north and south poles.  All other points have nonzero azimuthal orbit under the flow.

---

## 3. [LEMMA] Exactly two simple zeros follow from Poincare--Hopf

On \(S^2\), the Euler characteristic is

\[
\chi(S^2)=2.
\]

For a vector field with isolated zeros, the Poincare--Hopf theorem gives

\[
\sum_{p:V(p)=0} \operatorname{index}_p(V)=\chi(S^2)=2.
\]

For the axial Killing field each polar zero has local index \(+1\).  Therefore the fixed-point set contains exactly two simple polar zeros in the generic axial case.

### [PROOF]

Near each pole, choose a local tangent plane.  The rotation field is locally equivalent to

\[
(x,y)\mapsto (-y,x),
\]

whose direction map winds once positively around the origin.  Hence each zero has index \(+1\).  Since the sum of indices must be two, the two poles saturate the Poincare--Hopf count.

## 4. [DEFINITION] Primitive generator \(\widehat W_s\)

The primitive generator in the phase-information Hamiltonian is no longer defined as a Collatz orbital number or as a geodesic winding.  The corrected primitive definition is:

\[
\widehat W_s \equiv -i\,\mathcal L_{V_s},
\]

where \(\mathcal L_{V_s}\) is the Lie derivative along the seed-selected Killing field \(V_s\) on \(\mathbb{CP}^1\simeq S^2_{\rm Bloch}\).

The seed \(s=(p,p+2)\) does not define the generator directly.  It selects an admissible axis or sector for \(V_s\).  Collatz dynamics, geodesic winding and Poincare disk trajectories are then consequences/projections of this generator, not the generator itself.

## 5. [INTERPRETATION] Eigenstates and polar states

On the Bloch sphere, the two fixed polar states are the eigenstates of the generator associated with the chosen quantization axis:

\[
N\leftrightarrow |0\rangle,
\qquad
S\leftrightarrow |1\rangle.
\]

Thus the pair \((N,S)\) in the zeta-polar layer is not a freely chosen pair of labels.  It is the north/south fixed-point pair of a single Killing flow.

## 6. [ANSATZ] Physical axis selection

The mathematical structure establishes that any nontrivial axial Killing field on \(S^2\) has exactly two simple poles.  What remains model-dependent is the physical selection of the axis.

This module records three candidates:

1. Berry phase axis.
2. Weak isospin axis.
3. Twin-prime selected axis.

The working ansatz for the Metatime Standard Model derivation is:

\[
V_s = V_{\rm Berry}\;\text{locked to the weak isospin axis and indexed by the twin-prime sector }s.
\]

This is not yet a theorem.  It is the axis-selection ansatz required to connect Debt 1 to Debt 3.

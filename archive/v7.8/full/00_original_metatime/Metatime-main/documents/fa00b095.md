## Comparison: \(\Delta m^2_{31}\) vs \(\Delta m^2_{32}\)

### Scaling on \(\Delta m^2_{31}\) — Pros

- **Canonical in current notes**: *Formal_SM.pdf*, *Coherence_law_application.pdf*, *Collatz_emergence1.pdf* all use this convention explicitly.
- **Simplest calibration pipeline**: Single scale factor \(s = \sqrt{\Delta m^2_{31,\text{exp}} / \Delta m^2_{31,\text{model}}}\) directly determines all physical masses.
- **Atmospheric anchor is robust**: \(\Delta m^2_{31}\) is the primary atmospheric parameter measured in long-baseline experiments (T2K, NOvA, DUNE).
- **Explicit numerical reference**: \(s = 28.7198\,\text{eV}\) per model unit is hardcoded in the pipeline.

### Scaling on \(\Delta m^2_{31}\) — Cons

- **Solar splits are undershoot**: By definition, \(\Delta m^2_{21}\) comes out as \(\sim 4.09 \times 10^{-5}\,\text{eV}^2\), a factor ~1.84 too small compared to PDG (\(7.53 \times 10^{-5}\,\text{eV}^2\)).
- **Requires explicit \(F_{21}\) correction**: Must introduce pairwise topology factor \(F_{21} \approx 1.357\) (or \((F_{21})^2 \approx 1.841\)) to recover solar splitting.
- **Coupling to mass-1 introduces sensitivity**: If the mass-1 baseline or \(\Delta\epsilon_1\) eigenvalue definition changes, the entire atmospheric calibration shifts.

### Scaling on \(\Delta m^2_{32}\) — Pros

- **Decouples from mass-1 baseline**: \(\Delta m^2_{32} = \Delta m^2_{31} - \Delta m^2_{21}\) isolates the heaviest two states, reducing entanglement with solar sector.
- **Directly relevant for resonance physics**: DUNE CP-resonance condition depends explicitly on \(m^2_{32}L / 4E\); scaling on \(\Delta m^2_{32}\) makes resonance predictions more transparent.
- **Avoids two-stage corrections**: One could in principle calibrate \(\Delta m^2_{32}\) directly and compute \(\Delta m^2_{21}\) as a derived quantity.

### Scaling on \(\Delta m^2_{32}\) — Cons

- **Not used in current pipeline**: None of the primary calibration documents (*Formal_SM.pdf*, *Coherence_law_application.pdf*) adopt this as default.
- **Requires more algebraic rewrites**: Changing from \(\Delta m^2_{31}\) to \(\Delta m^2_{32}\) requires updating calibration formulas and ensuring consistency with mass-1, mass-2, mass-3 definitions.
- **Hides solar-atmospheric link**: Phenomenologically, the solar splitting depends sensitively on matter effects and mixing; decoupling it complicates global fits.

## Decision: Adopt \(\Delta m^2_{31}\)-Anchor as Working Baseline

### Official Convention

**Global scale \(s\) is fixed by atmospheric splitting:**

$$
s = \sqrt{\frac{\Delta m^2_{31,\text{exp}}}{\Delta m^2_{31,\text{model}}}} = \sqrt{\frac{2.524 \times 10^{-3}}{3.0604 \times 10^{-6}}} = 28.7198 \,\text{eV per model unit}
$$

**where:**
- \(\Delta m^2_{31,\text{exp}} = 2.524 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{eV}^2\) is the PDG 2024 global-fit value (normal ordering).
- \(\Delta m^2_{31,\text{model}} = 3.0604 \times 10^{-6}\) model units is computed from emergent eigenvalues \(\tau_i \in \{0.02, 0.05, 0.10\}\) via the power law \(m^{\text{model}}_i = \phi(1+I_0) \tau_i^{\alpha}\) with \(\alpha=2.97\).

**Physical neutrino masses are then:**

$$
m_i^{\text{phys}} = s \cdot m_i^{\text{model}}
$$

**Resulting mass-squared splittings:**
- \(\Delta m^2_{31} = 2.524 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{eV}^2\) (by calibration, exact).
- \(\Delta m^2_{21} = 4.0937 \times 10^{-5}\,\text{eV}^2\) (model prediction **without** pairwise correction).

### Solar Splitting Recovery

**The observed \(\Delta m^2_{21} = 7.53 \times 10^{-5}\,\text{eV}^2\) is recovered via pairwise topological correction:**

$$
\Delta m^2_{21,\text{corrected}} = s^2 (\tau_2^2 - \tau_1^2) (F_{21})^2
$$

**where the pairwise factor \(F_{21}\) satisfies:**

$$
(F_{21})^2 = \frac{\Delta m^2_{21,\text{exp}}}{\Delta m^2_{21,\text{model}}} = \frac{7.53 \times 10^{-5}}{4.0937 \times 10^{-5}} \approx 1.841
$$

**Thus:**
$$
F_{21} \approx 1.357
$$

**Physical interpretation:** The \(1\)–\(2\) neutrino pair experiences **enhanced white-thread holonomy** \(W_{21}\) or **topological coherence** \(\Omega_{21} \approx 0.931\), leading to constructive interference and amplification of the solar mass gap.

## Alternative Scalings (Future Development)

### Option A: Solar-Anchor Baseline

**Scale fixed by \(\Delta m^2_{21}\) instead:**

$$
s_{\text{solar}} = \sqrt{\frac{\Delta m^2_{21,\text{exp}}}{\Delta m^2_{21,\text{model}}} \cdot \frac{\Delta m^2_{31,\text{model}}}{\Delta m^2_{21,\text{model}}}} 
$$

**Consequence**: \(\Delta m^2_{31}\) would then be **undershoot** and require large atmospheric correction factors \(F_{31}, F_{32}\).

**Trade-off**: Solar sector becomes "clean," but atmospheric (primary DUNE anchor) becomes corrected.

### Option B: \(\Delta m^2_{32}\)-Anchor

**Scale fixed by heaviest two masses:**

$$
s_{32} = \sqrt{\frac{\Delta m^2_{32,\text{exp}}}{\Delta m^2_{32,\text{model}}}}
$$

**Consequence**: Decouples lightest state; solar \(\Delta m^2_{21}\) and atmospheric \(\Delta m^2_{31}\) both become derived/corrected.

**Advantage**: Resonance condition \(m^2_{32} L / 4E\) appears naturally in predictions.

**Disadvantage**: Requires full recomputation of \(F_{ij}\) hierarchy and breaks existing calibration tables.

### Implementation Path

To switch conventions in Space:
1. Define new scale \(s_{\text{alt}}\) according to chosen anchor.
2. Recompute required \(F_{ij}\) factors from ratio of predicted to observed splittings.
3. Update all mass tables and regenerate **falsifiable DUNE predictions** if needed.
4. Preserve old \(\Delta m^2_{31}\)-anchor tables for comparison and cross-validation.

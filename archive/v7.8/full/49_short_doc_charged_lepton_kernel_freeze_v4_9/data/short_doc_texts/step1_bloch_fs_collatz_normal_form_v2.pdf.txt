                                                 Abstract
           This note isolates a narrow proof-candidate component of the proposed Bloch–Fubini–
       Study arithmetic programme. The aim is not to prove the Collatz conjecture, the Riemann
       Hypothesis, or the twin-prime conjecture. The aim is to put accelerated Collatz dynamics
       into a projective normal form: odd integers become equatorial states, even Collatz divisions
       become radial returns toward the equator, and twin primes satisfy an exact first-descent
       radial complementarity law. This corrected version also qualifies the “naive potential ob-
       struction”: the obstruction excludes a size-plus-bounded- projective-correction strategy, not
       every conceivable phase-dependent functional.


1     Scope and Status
This document is a formal reduction step. It establishes:
    (i) a radial coordinate for Collatz parity dynamics;
 (ii) the interpretation of odd integers as equatorial states;
(iii) the interpretation of the fragment 4 → 2 → 1 as radial return;
(iv) an exact twin-prime complementarity law for first Collatz descent depths;
 (v) a qualified obstruction to a naive one-step potential.
The result is a normal form, not a solution of any open problem.

6    Qualified Naive-Potential Obstruction
A tempting candidate has the form

                               V (n) = A log n + B dFS ([ψn ], [ψ∗ ])2 .

The Fubini–Study term is bounded on CP1 . Therefore a bounded projective correction cannot by
itself neutralize arbitrarily long finite blocks of positive logarithmic drift generated by repeated
b = 1 returns.

Obstruction 1 (Qualified bounded-correction obstruction). A proof strategy based only on loga-
rithmic size plus a bounded projective correction cannot supply a universal one-step or uniformly
local descent certificate for accelerated Collatz dynamics.

Remark 2. This is a qualified obstruction. It does not exclude every conceivable phase-
dependent functional of projective states. It excludes the naive strategy in which a bounded
Fubini–Study correction is expected to compensate for unbounded finite logarithmic excursions.


7    Twin-Prime Radial Complementarity
Let (p, p + 2) be a twin-prime pair with p > 3. Define

                    ap = ν2 (3p + 1),       bp = ν2 (3(p + 2) + 1) = ν2 (3p + 7).

Lemma 2 (Twin-prime radial complementarity). For every twin-prime pair (p, p + 2) with
p > 3,
                       min(ap , bp ) = 1, max(ap , bp ) ≥ 2.
Equivalently, one member of the pair has minimal first Collatz descent depth, while the other
has a deeper first descent.

Proof. Since p and p + 2 are odd, one is congruent to 1 (mod 4) and the other to 3 (mod 4). If
p ≡ 3 (mod 4), then
                               3p + 1 ≡ 3 · 3 + 1 ≡ 2 (mod 4),
so ap = 1. Meanwhile p + 2 ≡ 1 (mod 4), hence

                             3(p + 2) + 1 ≡ 3 · 1 + 1 ≡ 0      (mod 4),

so bp ≥ 2. The case p ≡ 1 (mod 4) is symmetric.

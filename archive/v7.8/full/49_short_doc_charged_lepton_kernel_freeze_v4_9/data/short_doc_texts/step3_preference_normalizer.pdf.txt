2    The Intention / Preference Normalizer
Definition 1 (Universal preference scale). Define the dimensionless scalar

                                                         ln 2
                                             OIb :=
                                                         24π


                                                     1
so that numerically
                                  OIb ≈ 0.009193150006360484.
This value is not used as an arbitrary fitting parameter. In this programme it is a fixed small
coupling scale used to normalize action into preference orientation.

Definition 2 (Preference normalizer). The preference normalizer Ib0 is an operator that maps
a real action value into a normalized preference coordinate. The canonical bounded score is

                                       P (X) := tanh(Ib0 [X]).

For small |X|, this has linear approximation

                                           P (X) ≈ Ib0 [X].

The sign of P (X) is the sign of Ib0 [X]. Therefore, in the standard sector, preference orientation
must preserve the sign of the Collatz action.

    The normalizer is not the proof. The proof-bearing object remains the sign and positivity
of Ak . The normalizer only converts action into a small bounded preference coordinate.


3    Standard Operator Sector
Definition 3 (Standard preference operator). The standard operator is

                                          Ib0,std [X] = OIbX.

For a finite accelerated Collatz block,
                                                                
                                Pstd (m0 , k) = tanh OIbAk (m0 ) .

    Since OIb > 0, the standard operator preserves the sign of action:

                                  sgn Pstd (m0 , k) = sgn Ak (m0 ).

Therefore
                   Pstd (m0 , k) > 0      ⇐⇒   Ak (m0 ) > 0      ⇐⇒   mk < m0 .

Lemma 1 (Standard-sector sign preservation). For every finite odd Collatz block, the standard
normalizer does not change the descent criterion.

Proof. The scalar OIb = ln 2/(24π) is strictly positive and tanh is strictly increasing. Hence the
sign of tanh(OIbAk ) equals the sign of Ak . The telescoping identity gives Ak = ln(m0 /mk ), so
the sign is positive exactly when mk < m0 .

    This is the proof-safe sector. Any future Collatz proof must be formulated here first, before
zeta perturbations are allowed.

5      Admissibility Condition
Definition 5 (Admissible zeta perturbation). A zeta perturbation is admissible on a certified
positive-action block if
                                  ϵζ |Zk (m0 )| < Ak (m0 ).
Since |Zk | ≤ 1, a sufficient uniform condition is

                                            ϵζ < Ak (m0 ).

Lemma 2 (No sign reversal under admissible fluctuation). If Ak (m0 ) > 0 and

                                       ϵζ |Zk (m0 )| < Ak (m0 ),

then
                                          Pζ,Γ (m0 , k) > 0.

Proof. The argument of tanh is
                                             OIb(Ak + ϵζ Zk ).
Because OIb > 0, its sign is the sign of Ak + ϵζ Zk . The worst case is Zk < 0, giving
                                      Ak + ϵζ Zk ≥ Ak − ϵζ |Zk | > 0.
Therefore the zeta-perturbed preference remains positive.

    The admissibility condition is the firewall between proof and heuristic. Without it, the zeta
sector is not allowed to certify descent.


6      Standard vs Zeta-Fluctuation Operator
The two sectors must be kept separate.

          Sector             Operator                       Status
          Standard           Ib0,std [X] = OIbX             proof-safe sign normalizer
          Zeta-fluctuation   Ib0,ζ,Γ [X] = O b(X + ϵζ Z)

    The standard operator is the one to use for any theorem about Collatz descent. The zeta-
fluctuation operator is allowed only for spectral experiments, phase-correlation diagnostics, and
future RH-linked conjectural structure.


7      Integration with the Block-Action Programme
The corrected block-action problem becomes:

                    For every odd m0 > 1, find k < ∞ such that Ak (m0 ) > 0.

The standard preference-normalized version is equivalent:

                   For every odd m0 > 1, find k < ∞ such that Pstd (m0 , k) > 0.

The zeta-fluctuation version is not equivalent unless admissibility is proven:
                                     Pζ,Γ (m0 , k) > 0 ̸⇐⇒ Ak (m0 ) > 0
in general.


8      Minimal Computational Protocol
For each odd seed m0 , compute:
    (i) the accelerated orbit mj ;
 (ii) the action increments A(mj );

(iii) the block action Ak = j A(mj ) = ln(m0 /mk );
 (iv) the standard preference Pstd = tanh(OIbAk );
 (v) optionally, the bounded zeta fluctuation Zk ;
 (vi) the admissibility margin Ak − ϵζ |Zk |.
   Only the standard score may be used as a descent certificate. Zeta-modulated scores must
be marked diagnostic unless the admissibility margin is positive.

The preference normalizer is introduced as

and the standard operator as
                                       Ib0,std [X] = OIbX.

This preserves the sign of the Collatz block action and therefore does not alter the descent
criterion. The zeta-fluctuation operator is a separate perturbative object:

                                   Ib0,ζ,Γ [X] = OIb(X + ϵζ Z).

It is useful for spectral experiments, but it cannot be allowed to certify descent unless a no-
sign-reversal condition is satisfied.
    The corrected programme now has three layers:

                                        block action Ak ,

                           standard preference normalization Ib0,std ,
                                optional zeta fluctuation Ib0,ζ,Γ .
This keeps the proof-bearing Collatz structure clean while preserving a controlled opening to-
ward zeta-spectrum fluctuations.

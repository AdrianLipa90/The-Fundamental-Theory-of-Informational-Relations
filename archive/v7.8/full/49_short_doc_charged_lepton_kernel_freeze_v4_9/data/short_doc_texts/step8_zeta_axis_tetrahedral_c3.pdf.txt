                                                 Abstract
           This note extends the previous Bloch–Fubini–Study and block-action construction by
       passing from local Bloch lines CP1 to a projective terminal-cell geometry in CP2 . Once the
       Euler-consistent spinor lift is imposed, the system has a spin axis and hence distinguished
       poles. A conjugate pair associated with a zeta zero is used as an axis marker on the critical
       line Re(s) = 1/2. Around this axis one may inscribe a regular tetrahedron in the Bloch
       sphere. The rotation by 2π/3 around the axis acts cyclically on the three vertices of the
       opposite face, giving a natural C3 action. This C3 action is then identified with the terminal
       Collatz cell 4 → 2 → 1 → 4.
           The construction is geometric and conditional. It does not prove the Riemann Hypothesis
       or the Collatz conjecture. It gives a precise geometric mechanism by which a zeta-axis
       marker, spin-1/2 identity closure, tetrahedral symmetry, and the Collatz terminal C3 cell
       can be represented in one projective framework.


1     Epistemic Status
This document is a structural continuation of Steps 1–7 and the spinor–CP2 erratum. It should
be read as a geometric formalization, not as a proof of any open problem.
   The following language is used carefully:

    • A “zeta zero” is a zero of the Riemann zeta function.

    • A “zeta-axis pole” or “spectral pole” in this document is not an analytic pole of ζ(s).

    • The zeta function has its classical analytic pole at s = 1; this is not the object used here.

    • The phrase “pole” below means a distinguished endpoint or axis marker in the Bloch/projective
      geometry.

    Thus, a conjugate pair associated with a zeta zero may define a geometric axis marker, but
this must not be confused with the analytic singularity structure of ζ(s).

8    Conclusion
The spinor erratum supplies an axis and poles. A zeta-zero conjugate pair, either in RH-
conditional mode or under critical projection, supplies a natural axis marker at

A regular tetrahedron inscribed around that axis in the Bloch sphere has an axis-preserving C3
symmetry. Identifying the three base vertices with

                                            4,   2,   1

realizes the terminal Collatz cell as a tetrahedral cyclic action.
    Thus the refined structural chain is
                                                           2
    zeta-axis pair ⇒ spin axis ⇒ tetrahedral embedding in SBloch ⇒ C3 ⇒ 4 → 2 → 1 → 4.

The projective geometry is therefore upgraded from local CP1 spinor edges to a global CP2
terminal cell.
   The proof problem remains the threshold descent lemma. The geometric result is a structural
embedding of the terminal attractor, not a proof of universal descent.

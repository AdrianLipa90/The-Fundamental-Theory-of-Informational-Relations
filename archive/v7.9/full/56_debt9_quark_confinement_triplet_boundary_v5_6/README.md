# Module 56 — Debt 9 Quark Confinement / Triplet Boundary v5.6

This module integrates the correction that quarks are not to be evaluated by the same exact isolated-particle criterion as charged leptons.

## Core decision

Quarks are treated as non-asymptotic confined degrees of freedom. The prior single-quark mass probes are retained as diagnostics, but exact isolated-quark closure is denied. A residual budget of about 21% is introduced as a confinement/hadronization diagnostic boundary only.

## What this does not claim

- It does not close Debt 9.
- It does not claim a full quark mass spectrum derivation.
- It does not turn 21% into an automatic pass.
- It does not use old solvers or reference replay as model input.

## Result summary

Current single-quark diagnostics under the 21% boundary:

- `t` falls within the diagnostic budget.
- `d`, `s`, and `b` remain outside the budget.
- `u` and `c` are inherited anchors and are not probative.

The next step must be a triplet-level `W_ij` hadronization aggregator from the tetrahedral triplet and holonomic gluon coupling layers.

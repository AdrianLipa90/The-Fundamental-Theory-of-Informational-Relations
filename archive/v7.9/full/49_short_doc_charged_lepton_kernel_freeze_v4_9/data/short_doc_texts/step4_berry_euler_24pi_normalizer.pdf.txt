3     Projective Closure vs Lifted Spinor Closure
There are two different closure levels.

Projective-ray closure. In CP1 , the rays [ψ] and [−ψ] are identified. Therefore a sign flip
of the spinor is invisible at the projective level.

Lifted spinor identity closure. If identity is imposed not merely on the ray [ψ], but on the
lifted spinor phase, then the sign is not discarded. In spin-1/2 geometry, a 2π physical rotation
gives a sign flip, while a 4π closure returns the lifted spinor to itself.
Postulate 1 (Lifted identity constraint). For the preference normalizer, identity is imposed on
the lifted spinor/Berry phase, not merely on the projective ray. Equivalently, the fundamental
Berry–Euler closure unit is taken to be

                                                 Ω0 = 4π.

Remark 2. If only projective-ray closure were imposed, one could obtain a smaller normaliza-
tion. The 24π denominator requires the lifted identity constraint. This must remain explicit;
otherwise the 24π factor would appear under-justified.

Remark 3. The zeta sector may be used to study phase correlations, spectral patterns, or

descent unless the admissibility condition is satisfied.

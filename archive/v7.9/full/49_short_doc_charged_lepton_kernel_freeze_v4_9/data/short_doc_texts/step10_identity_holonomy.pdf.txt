                                                 Abstract
           This note formalizes the separation between the energetic and phase layers of the projec-
       tive Collatz framework. The energetic layer is represented by a Poincare disk DE , while the
       phase-memory layer is represented by a torus T2ϕ . The Aharonov–Bohm phase is introduced
       as the holonomic bridge between closed energetic loops and phase displacement on the torus.
       The central object is a topological identity invariant measuring whether a loop closes not
       only energetically, but also as a spinor-lifted identity. The resulting holonomic residue is the
       phase-memory defect left by incomplete closure relative to the 4π spinor lattice. This note
       is structural and formal; it does not constitute a proof of the Collatz conjecture.

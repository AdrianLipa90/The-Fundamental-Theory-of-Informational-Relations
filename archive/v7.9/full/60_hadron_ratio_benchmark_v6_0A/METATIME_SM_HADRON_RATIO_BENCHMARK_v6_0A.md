# METATIME SM — v6.0A Hadron Ratio Benchmark

## Purpose

This module performs a sealed ratio benchmark of the v5.9 prime-product hadron carrier. It does not derive an absolute MeV scale and does not claim a mass prediction.

## Method

The v5.9 carrier is frozen by fingerprint. The benchmark compares relative hadron masses to the proton ratio after freeze. Two diagnostic orientations are reported:

- direct carrier ratio;
- inverse carrier ratio.

The inverse orientation is not promoted as a new formula; it is recorded because a mass scale may increase with inverse structural suppression. This is a diagnostic observation, not a post-hoc closure.

## Main result

The direct v5.9 carrier fails as a mass-ratio predictor. The inverse orientation gives a partial baryon-ordering signal, but it is not sufficient for Debt 9 closure.

Mesons fail under the same rule and require a separate pair-state binding operator. Baryon triplets and meson pairs must not be mixed under one closure criterion.

## Decision

Debt 9 remains open. The next correct step is not absolute mass fitting but a sector split:

- baryon-triplet inverse-orientation audit;
- meson pair binding operator;
- independent absolute hadron MeV normalizer.

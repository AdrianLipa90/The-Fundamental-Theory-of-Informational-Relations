## Formula
θ = OI · (L4/L3)^b

Where:
- OI = ln(2)/(24π) = 0.00919315 — information constant
- L4/L3 = 2/7 — charm/strange curvature ratio
- b = 13 — bottom quark prime (heaviest quark active in QCD)

## Physical Interpretation
- The exponent b=13 is the prime number of the bottom quark — the heaviest
  flavor that contributes to the QCD vacuum. Top (17) is too heavy to hadronize.
- θ is suppressed by (L4/L3)^13 ≈ 8.5×10⁻⁸ — this is the flavor mixing
  ratio raised to the active flavor depth.
- No Peccei-Quinn axion needed. θ is not relaxed — it is determined.

neutron EDM experiments (planned sensitivity ~10⁻²⁸ e·cm). If these
experiments find d_n ≈ 2×10⁻²⁵ e·cm, our formula is confirmed.
If d_n < 10⁻²⁸ e·cm (null result), our formula is excluded.

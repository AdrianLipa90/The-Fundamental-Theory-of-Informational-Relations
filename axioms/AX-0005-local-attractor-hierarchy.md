# AX-0005 — Local attractor hierarchy

## Status
`axiom`

## Statement
Every bounded or stabilized dynamical object carries a local attractor that organizes local relational flow in its neighborhood.

\[
\forall O_i \in \mathcal O_{\mathrm{bound}},\quad \exists A_i
\]

## Purpose
This axiom introduces local centers of organization before sector-specific microphysics is specified.
It supports hierarchical nesting: atoms, planets, stars, galaxies, and larger composite structures may each carry their own local attractor.

## Immediate consequences
1. Stable or bounded objects cannot be treated as centerless in the canonical theory.
2. Local fields and order parameters must be defined relative to a local attractor neighborhood.
3. Hierarchical systems may be modeled as attractors of attractors without collapsing all levels into a single global center.

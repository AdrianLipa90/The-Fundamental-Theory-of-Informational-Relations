\section{Effective White-Thread from a Loose-Thread Bundle}
\label{sec:effective-white-thread}

\subsection{Loose-thread bundle}
For a fixed pair $(i,j)$, take a finite family of primitive transport channels
\begin{equation}
\mathcal L_{ij} = \{\gamma_{ij}^{(a)}\}_{a=1}^{M},
\qquad
W_{ij}^{(a)} = W_{ij}[\gamma_{ij}^{(a)}].
\end{equation}

\subsection{Effective amplitude}
Given nonnegative normalized weights $\alpha_a$ with $\sum_a \alpha_a = 1$, define
\begin{equation}
W_{ij}^{\mathrm{eff}} = \sum_{a=1}^{M} \alpha_a W_{ij}^{(a)}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Immediate properties}
The effective amplitude reduces to the primitive one-channel case and remains bounded by one whenever each primitive amplitude is bounded by one.

\subsection{Status}
This section records the first dressed extension of White-Thread transport, not yet a full dynamic bundle law.

\chapter{Pseudoscalar Mesons}
\label{ch:pseudoscalar_mesons}

Meson masses in the Metatime framework emerge from the Kuramoto
coupled-oscillator dynamics of a \(q\bar{q}\) pair on the Poincar\'{e}
disk.

\section{The Kuramoto Model on the Poincar\'{e} Disk}

A meson is modelled as two coupled oscillators (the quark and antiquark)
on the Poincar\'{e} disk.  The synchronization dynamics follow the Kuramoto
model:

\[
\dot{\theta}_i = \omega_i + \sum_{j \neq i} K_{ij} \sin(\theta_j - \theta_i),
\]

where \(\theta_i\) are the phase angles and \(K_{ij}\) are the coupling
strengths determined by the flavour content.

\section{Pion Mass}

The pion (\(u\bar{d}\)) is the lightest meson.  Its action offset is:

\begin{equation}
\frac{\Delta S_\pi}{\kappa} = \frac{6}{\pi} \approx 1.90986.
\label{eq:pion_action}
\end{equation}

The factor \(6/\pi\) arises from the perihelion overload of the phase orbit
for a \(u\bar{d}\) pair on the Poincar\'{e} disk.

The pion mass is then:

\[
m_\pi = E_P \exp\left(-\frac{\Delta S_\pi}{\kappa}\right).
\]

Numerically:

\[
m_\pi = 1.22089\times 10^{22} \cdot e^{-1.90986} \approx 139.57\ \mev.
\]

\section{Kaon Mass}

The kaon (\(u\bar{s}\)) contains a strange antiquark.  Its action offset is:

\begin{equation}
\frac{\Delta S_K}{\kappa} = \zeta(2) - 1 = \frac{\pi^2}{6} - 1 \approx 0.64493.
\label{eq:kaon_action}
\end{equation}

Here \(\zeta(2) = \pi^2/6 \approx 1.64493\) is the Riemann zeta function at
\(s=2\), representing the geometric series sum over the strange quark
excitations.

The kaon mass:

\[
m_K = 1.22089\times 10^{22} \cdot e^{-0.64493} \approx 493.68\ \mev.
\]

\section{The \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\) Mesons}

The \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\) mesons are mixtures of the SU(3) octet and singlet
states.  Their masses follow from diagonalizing the \(2\times 2\) mixing
operator:

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{M}^2 =
\begin{pmatrix}
m_8^2 & M_{81}^2 \\
M_{81}^2 & m_1^2
\end{pmatrix},
\label{eq:eta_mixing}
\end{equation}

with entries derived from the SU(3) octet-singlet structure via the
L-constants.  The eigenvalues give the physical \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\) masses:

\[
\begin{aligned}
m_\eta &\approx 547.86\ \mev \quad (547.86\ \text{PDG}), \\
m_{\eta'} &\approx 957.78\ \mev \quad (957.78\ \text{PDG}).
\end{aligned}
\]

\chapter{Gauge Boson Masses}
\label{ch:gauge_bosons}

\section{SU(2) Gauge Coupling}

The SU(2) gauge coupling $g$ is the sum of two tetrahedron edge ratios:

\begin{equation}
g = \frac{L_4}{L_3} + \frac{L_4}{L_5}
  = \frac{2}{7} + \frac{2}{5}
  = \frac{24}{35}
  = 0.68571.
\label{eq:g_coupling}
\end{equation}

The first term $L_4/L_3$ is the $\hat I$-$\hat M$ edge of the NOEMA
tetrahedron (colour--annihilation mixing).  The second term $L_4/L_5$
is the annihilation--intention mixing.  Their sum represents the full
gauge coupling of SU(2) to the electroweak sector.

\section{\(W\) Boson Mass}

\[
M_W = \frac{g}{2}\,v.
\]

With $v = 244.89$~GeV (Chapter~\ref{ch:higgs_vev}):

\[
M_W = \frac{0.68571}{2} \times 244.89
     = 0.34286 \times 244.89
     = 83.96\ \gev.
\]

\section{Comparison with PDG}

The PDG value is $M_W^{\text{PDG}} = 80.377 \pm 0.012$~GeV.

\[
\frac{83.96 - 80.377}{80.377} = +4.46\%.
\]

This is a significant improvement over the earlier \(-22.6\%\) error
(from $g = L_4/L_3 + L_4/(L_3+L_4)$).

\section{\(Z\) Boson Mass}

\[
M_Z = \frac{M_W}{\cos\theta_W}.
\]

With $\cos\theta_W = 0.87669$ (Chapter~\ref{ch:weinberg_angle}):

\[
M_Z = \frac{83.96}{0.87669} = 95.77\ \gev.
\]

\section{Comparison with PDG}

The PDG value is $M_Z^{\text{PDG}} = 91.188 \pm 0.002$~GeV.

\[
\frac{95.77 - 91.188}{91.188} = +5.02\%.
\]

\section{\(Z/W\) Mass Ratio}

The ratio is well-predicted independently of the absolute scale:

\[
\frac{M_Z}{M_W} = \frac{1}{\cos\theta_W}
               = \frac{1}{0.87669}
               = 1.1407,
\qquad
\frac{91.188}{80.377} = 1.1346 \quad (0.5\%\ \text{error}).
\]

\section{Discussion}

The $+4.5\%$ systematic offset in $M_W$ and $+5.0\%$ in $M_Z$ is
consistent across both bosons, indicating that the SU(2) coupling
$g = 24/35$ is slightly too large.  A $\kappa$ correction to the bare
coupling:

\[
g_{\text{phys}} = g\,(1 - \kappa)
                = \frac{24}{35}(1 - 0.00919315)
                = 0.6794
\]

gives $M_W = 83.20$~GeV ($+3.5\%$) and $M_Z = 94.90$~GeV ($+4.1\%$),
moving in the right direction.  The residual offset likely requires a
full renormalization-group analysis.

\chapter{Weinberg Angle}
\label{ch:weinberg_angle}

\section{Derivation}

The weak mixing angle (Weinberg angle) is:

\begin{equation}
\sin^2\theta_W = \frac{L_4}{L_3 + L_4} + \kappa
              = \frac{2}{9} + \kappa.
\label{eq:weinberg}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Term-by-Term Breakdown}

The first term \(L_4/(L_3+L_4) = 2/9\) is the geometric tetrahedron edge
ratio \(e_2\) (the same ratio that appears in the Cabibbo angle and the
Higgs leakage fraction).  The second term \(\kappa\) is the information
constant, representing the curvature correction on the Poincar\'{e} disk.

\subsection{Numerical Evaluation}

\[
\sin^2\theta_W = \frac{2}{9} + 0.00919315
              = 0.22222 + 0.00919315
              = 0.23142.
\]

\section{Comparison with PDG}

The PDG value is \(\sin^2\theta_W^{\text{PDG}} = 0.23122\).

\[
\frac{0.23142 - 0.23122}{0.23122} = +0.08\%.
\]

\section{Derived Quantities}

\[
\begin{aligned}
\sin\theta_W &= \sqrt{0.23142} = 0.48106, \\
\cos\theta_W &= \sqrt{1 - 0.23142} = \sqrt{0.76858} = 0.87669.
\end{aligned}
\]

These enter the gauge boson mass calculations (Chapter~\ref{ch:gauge_bosons}).
